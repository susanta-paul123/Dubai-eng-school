House System

As well as the opportunity for our students to participate in external events with other Dubai Schools, we also have many inter-school competitions where our students compete against each other in our House System. When joining The English College, one of the most reputed UK curriculum schools in Dubai, all students will be allocated to one of our four houses of Eagle, Falcon, Kestrel or Hawk. The house competitions are vast and range from Swimming Galas to Spelling Bees. We really do have a competition for all and we encourage all students to represent their house in at least one event, whether that be in sports, academics or the arts.

The Duke of Edinburgh’s International Award

The Duke of Edinburgh’s International Award is the world’s leading youth achievement award, bringing together practical experiences and life skills to equip young people for life.

We are very proud to offer the prestigious International Award (better known as the Duke of Edinburgh award in the UK) to all of our students in Year 10 to Year 13. It is an award that is closely aligned with our core values of Aspiration, Gratitude, Integrity, Resilience and Respect.

Extra-curricular Activities

We support the philosophy that education is not limited to the classroom environment. Consequently, a varied and exciting range of extra-curricular activities are available to all students, supported by well qualified staff and excellent facilities. Activities are scheduled for before school, lunchtime and after school, with new activities appearing on a regular basis. The programme goes hand-in-hand with our sports and PE provision, often giving students the extra training or impetus they need to make the squad and represent the school. Our intention is to help your child grow as an individual and develop a range of interests, but also to become responsible and committed with skills that will be of value after school life.

Student Leadership

All of our students are potential future leaders and consequently we have developed a student leadership programme that allows all of our students, regardless of their age, to spend time developing and honing skills which are essential for effective leadership – humility, integrity and communication.

Our Secondary leadership programme includes roles related to developing teaching and learning, such as our subject champions, roles related to the House system, such as House captains and roles related to making change across the school, such as our Student Council. The pinnacle of a student’s career is being elected Head Girl or Head Boy of their school; these roles are incredibly important at The English College and give our senior students the opportunity to drive change within our school.

We encourage all students to take advantage of the many leadership opportunities available to them. Whether they excel in their academics, in sports or in the arts – there is a leadership opportunity available to them.
